About Tele-Friend

Pen-pal to the elderly program: This program focuses on combating issues of loneliness and social isolation in rural elderly populations. By partnering college students with a “pen-pal” elderly partner, students can engage in virtual discussions and activities to help provide companionship to those who need it most. This program focuses on combating issues of loneliness and social isolation in rural elderly populations.

Who is eligible?

Anyone above the age of 60, experiencing mild feelings of loneliness and isolation, and interested in connecting with a college student for a give-and-take learning experience.

Where does it take place?

The tele-friend program takes place virtually, over either video platforms, such as zoom, or phone calls. Northeastern students at the boston campus will be paired with older adults in rural regions of Maine.
