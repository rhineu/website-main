ABOUT THE FOUNDERS

Reshika Sai Devarajan
Founder

Hi everyone! My Reshika Sai Devarajan and I am a second year Health Science major at Northeastern University. As a native of the state of Maine, I have been deeply aware of the health disparities between the rural and urban populations of the state. After going on a rural immersion trip through Northeastern, I was able to learn more about the unique struggles rural residents face, especially older adults. One specific issue is that there is a lack of focus and resources on social isolation and loneliness in this population, which is why the idea of a Tele-Friend program came to mind, to help give older adults meaningful connections and form long-lasting companionships. After contacting my good friend Khoshna, we were able to create RHI and the Tele-Friend program to try to make a difference and better this dilemma. Some fun facts about me is that I was born in Canada and I love to read!

Khoshna Ande
Co-Founder

Hi! My name is Khoshna and I’m currently a second year behavioral neuroscience student. I helped create this organization because currently, there are not many groups that address the health and social issues of rural residents. I found that social isolation was an especially relevant issue to focus on as we slowly transition to our post-quarantine world, which is why when Reshika originally reached out about starting something that advocated for rural health, I was eager to jump on board. Some fun facts about me are that I have a dog and a guinea pig (I love animals!), I met Roger Federer once at the US Open, and I can play the piano :)

ABOUT THE ADVISORS

Dr. Mohammed, Advisor

Dr. Shan Mohammed is a Clinical Professor in the Department of Health Sciences and is the Assistant Dean for Diversity, Equity and Inclusion Educational and Student Initiatives at Bouvé College of Health Sciences. Dr. Mohammed is the founding director of the Master of Public Health Program in Urban Health and led the program from 2007-2017. He has taught courses on rural health, community and public health, urban public health, health and human rights, and communication skills. He is a board-certified family medicine physician and a fellow of the American Association of Family Physicians. Before joining Northeastern in 2007, he held appointments in the Department of Family Medicine and the Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics in the School of Medicine at Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland, Ohio.

Dr. Simmonds, Advisor

Kathy Simmonds, PhD, MPH, RN, WHNP-BC, FAAN is a clinical professor at the Roux Institute/Bouvé College of Health Sciences. Dr. Simmonds’ has worked as a clinician in a variety of community-based settings including community health centers, Planned Parenthood, a migrant health clinic, and most recently the Portland Community Free Clinic. From 1996-2007, she worked at the Abortion Access Project, where she directed the Reproductive Options Education Consortium for Nursing (ROE). She was a founding member of the national organization Reproductive Health in Nursing (now Nurses for Sexual and Reproductive Health). Dr. Simmonds is on the Steering Committee of the MGHIHP Center for Climate Change, Climate Justice, and Health, and co-chair of the Alliance for Nurses for Healthy Environments (AHNE) Global Climate Change Working Group. She is interested in the intersection of Reproductive Justice and Climate Justice, and has published numerous articles and book chapters on sexual and reproductive health, unintended pregnancy, abortion, and the impacts of climate change on health.   Dr. Simmonds received her undergraduate degree from Cornell University, Master’s in Science in Nursing from the MGH Institute of Health Professions, Master’s in Public Health from the Harvard School of Public Health, and PhD from the University of Rhode Island. From 2014-2016 she was a Jonas Scholar, and an inaugural member of the American Academy of Nursing/Jonas Health Policy Scholar program. In 2022, she was named as a fellow in the American Academy of Nursing. Kathy  lives on Peaks Island in the beautiful Casco Bay, with her husband, son, and a menagerie of furry and feathered creatures.
